1) The Nile crocodile can hold its breath underwater for up to _______ while waiting for prey.

a) 30 Minuted
b) 2 Hours
c) 5 Hours
d) 10 Minutes

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

b) 2 Hours

The Nile crocodile (Crocodylus niloticus) is an African crocodile, the largest freshwater predator in Africa, and may be considered the second largest extant reptile in the world, after the saltwater crocodile. Nile crocodiles normally dive for only a few minutes at a time, but can swim underwater for up to 30 minutes if threatened, and if they remain fully inactive they can hold their breath for up to 2 hours (which, as aforementioned, is due to the high levels of lactic acid in their blood).

2) Garrett McNamara holds the official record for the highest wave ever surfed in history. How tall was the wave?

a) 78 Feet
b) 50 Feet
c) 200 Feet
d) 100 Feet

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

a) 78 Feet

According to Guinness World Records, Garrett McNamara (towed by Andrew Cotton) surfed the tallest recorded wave in history at at an astounding 78 feet (23 meters) in 2011. The term ‘towed’ means that “the surfer is brought to the wave, enabling them to catch waves that would otherwise be too strong to be caught by paddling in.” McNamara's official record might not stand for long, others have been rumored to have surfed waves reaching a height of 100 feet, however until the rumors have been confirmed McNamara remains the champ.

3) What is the name of this star constellation?

a) The Big Dipper
b) Scorpius
c) Ursa Major
d) Orion's Belt

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

d) Orion's Belt

Orion’s Belt is one of the most familiar asterisms in the night sky, along with the Big Dipper and the Southern Cross. It is formed by three massive, bright stars located in our galaxy, in the direction of the constellation Orion, the Hunter: Alnilam, Alnitak and Mintaka. Two of the three stars are supergiants.

4) In what body of water is the deepest postbox located?

a) The Gulf of Mexico off the coast of Texas
b) Susami Bay off the coast of Japan
c) The Aegean Sea off the coast of Greece
d) The Bay of Bengal off the coast of India

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

b) Susami Bay off the coast of Japan

The world’s deepest postbox is just off the coast of Susami Bay in Japan. It is 10 metres under water, and is used by divers to send waterproof postcards to their friends and family. A clerk checks the mailbox regularly to ensure all the postcards get to their destination.

6) There are three countries in the world that are completely landlocked by only one other country. What are those three countries?

a) Madagascar, Portugal, and Egypt
b) New Jersey, Mongolia, and Russia
c) Lesotho, San Marino, and Vatican City
d) French Guiana, Swaziland, and Austria

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

c) Lesotho, San Marino, and Vatican City

Lesotho, San Marino, and Vatican City are the only countries to be surrounded by just one other country. Lesotho is landlocked within South Africa while San Marino and Vatican City are surrounded by Italy.

7) There are more plastic flamigos in the USA than there are real flamingos.

a) True
b) False

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

a) True

Did you know there are more plastic flamingos in the USA than real ones? Plastic flamingos are widely considered to be the stereotypical example of lawn kitsch – the American’s answer to garden dwarfs. Designed in 1957 by Don Featherstone while working for Union Products, the pink flamingo has gone from a piece of Florida exotica to a symbol of trash culture. Featherstone sculpted his 3-D flamingos from clay, working from photos of the birds in National Geographic.

8) How many official living languages are there in the world?

a) 78 Living Languages
b) 14396 Living Languages
c) 2930 Living Languages
d) 6909 Living Languages

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

d) 6909 Living Languages

The Ethnologue catalogue of world languages, which is one of the best linguistic resources, currently lists 6909 living languages. About 6% of them have more than a million speakers each, and collectively account for 94% of the world population.

9) The banana plant is the largest ___________ flowering plant.

a) Conifer
b) Herbaceous
c) Piperaceae
d) Embryophyta

THE CORRECT ANSWER:

b) Herbaceous

The banana plant is the largest herbaceous flowering plant. All the above-ground parts of a banana plant grow from a structure usually called a "corm". Plants are normally tall and fairly sturdy, and are often mistaken for trees, but what appears to be a trunk is actually a "false stem" or pseudostem.
